MAHARASHTRA RENT CONTROL ACT, 1999
(Maharashtra Act No. XVIII of 2000)

An Act to unify, consolidate and amend the law relating to the control of rents and repairs of certain premises, for the protection of tenants in the State of Maharashtra and for matters connected therewith or incidental thereto.

1. Short title, extent, commencement and application.
(1) This Act may be called the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999.
(2) It extends to the whole of the State of Maharashtra.
(3) It shall come into force on such date as the State Government may, by notification in the Official Gazette, appoint.
(4) This Act shall apply to all premises except those specifically exempted under Section 3.

2. Definitions.
In this Act, unless the context otherwise requires —
(a) "premises" means any building or part of a building let separately for use as a residence or for commercial use or for the purpose of carrying on any industry or business, and includes —
    (i) the garden, grounds, garages and outhouses, if any, appurtenant to such building or part of a building;
    (ii) any furniture supplied by the landlord for use in such building or part of a building;
(b) "landlord" means any person who, for the time being, is receiving, or is entitled to receive, rent in respect of any premises, and includes the agent, attorney, receiver, executor, administrator or successor of such person;
(c) "tenant" means any person by whom or on whose account rent is payable for any premises, and includes —
    (i) any member of the tenant's family residing with the tenant at the time of the tenant's death;
    (ii) any person to whom the interest of the tenant in the premises devolves by inheritance;
(d) "standard rent" means the rent calculated in accordance with the provisions of Section 7;
(e) "fair rent" means the rent determined by the Court under Section 11;
(f) "Controller" means any officer appointed by the State Government under Section 28 to perform the functions of the Controller under this Act.

3. Exemptions.
Nothing in this Act shall apply to —
(a) any premises belonging to the Government or a local authority or to any company or corporation owned or managed by the Government;
(b) any premises let out for residential purposes where the monthly rent exceeds fifteen thousand rupees, unless the tenancy was created before the commencement of this Act;
(c) any premises let on leave and licence basis for a period not exceeding sixty months, provided that such licence has been executed and registered;
(d) any premises constructed after the commencement of this Act in areas specified by the State Government by notification in the Official Gazette.

4. Provisions of Act not to apply in certain cases.
Provided that the provisions of this Act shall not apply to any tenancy created on or after the commencement of this Act in respect of premises where the monthly rent payable is more than three thousand five hundred rupees in a Municipal Corporation area and more than two thousand rupees in a Municipal Council area.

5. Protection of tenant against eviction.
(1) No landlord shall recover possession of any premises or any part thereof, notwithstanding the termination of the period of tenancy, except on one or more of the following grounds:
(a) that the tenant has not paid or tendered to the landlord the amount of standard rent or permitted increases, if any, lawfully payable by him for a period of six months;
(b) that the tenant has used the premises for a purpose other than that for which they were let;
(c) that the tenant has, without the prior written consent of the landlord, erected any permanent structure on the premises;
(d) that the tenant has, without the prior written consent of the landlord, assigned or sub-let the premises or any portion thereof;
(e) that the tenant has caused substantial damage to the premises;
(f) that the premises are required bona fide by the landlord for his own occupation or for the occupation of any person dependent on him, provided that the landlord does not have any reasonably suitable alternative accommodation;
(g) that the premises are required for the purpose of carrying out repairs which cannot be carried out without the premises being vacated.

6. Restriction on increase of rent.
(1) No landlord shall claim or receive any amount in excess of the standard rent or permitted increases in respect of any premises.
(2) The standard rent once fixed shall not be increased except as provided in Section 9.
(3) No landlord shall claim or receive any amount in lieu of or in addition to the rent, including any amount as premium, pugree, salami or any other consideration whatsoever.
(4) Provided that the landlord may receive a refundable deposit not exceeding three months' rent, subject to the condition that the same shall be refunded to the tenant at the time of vacating the premises.

7. Standard rent.
(1) For the purposes of this Act, the standard rent of any premises shall be —
(a) where the premises have been let on any date before the appointed date, the rent at which they were let on such date;
(b) where the premises have not been let at any time before the appointed date, the rent fixed by the Court having jurisdiction.
(2) In fixing the standard rent, the Court shall take into consideration the rental value of similar premises in the vicinity, the cost of construction of the premises, and the amenities and services provided.

8. Permitted increases.
The landlord shall be entitled to increase the standard rent —
(a) by an amount equal to fifteen per cent per annum of the cost incurred by him in carrying out repairs to the premises with the consent of the tenant;
(b) by an amount not exceeding four per cent per annum of the standard rent where there is an increase in Municipal taxes;
(c) where new amenities or services are provided to the tenant, by an amount as agreed between the landlord and tenant, subject to the approval of the Controller.

9. Maintenance and repairs.
(1) Every landlord shall be responsible for keeping the premises in good and tenantable repair and shall carry out all such repairs as may be necessary to keep the premises in such condition.
(2) The tenant shall be responsible for day-to-day maintenance and minor repairs of the premises.
(3) If the landlord fails to carry out repairs as required under sub-section (1), the tenant may —
    (a) apply to the Controller for an order directing the landlord to carry out such repairs; or
    (b) carry out such repairs himself and deduct the cost thereof from the rent payable.
(4) Where the tenant carries out repairs under clause (b) of sub-section (3), the deduction shall not exceed three months' rent in any one year.

10. Deposits.
(1) Any amount paid as deposit or advance rent by the tenant to the landlord shall be refunded by the landlord to the tenant within thirty days of the tenant vacating the premises.
(2) If the landlord fails to refund the deposit within the period specified in sub-section (1), he shall be liable to pay interest thereon at the rate of twelve per cent per annum from the date of vacating till the date of refund.
(3) The landlord shall not be entitled to deduct any amount from the deposit except for arrears of rent, damages caused to the premises by the tenant, or unpaid utility charges.

11. Fair rent.
(1) The tenant or the landlord may apply to the Court for fixation of fair rent in respect of any premises.
(2) On receipt of such application, the Court shall fix the fair rent of the premises after considering —
    (a) the rental value of similar premises in the vicinity;
    (b) the amenities and services provided;
    (c) the cost of construction and maintenance;
    (d) the dates when the premises were constructed and when they were last let.
(3) The fair rent so fixed shall be the standard rent for the purposes of this Act.

12. Service charges.
(1) In addition to the standard rent, the landlord may recover from the tenant charges for services actually provided, including —
    (a) water charges;
    (b) electricity for common areas;
    (c) conservancy charges;
    (d) lift maintenance charges.
(2) Such charges shall be based on actual cost and shall be supported by receipts or bills.
(3) No profit shall be charged by the landlord on such service charges.

13. Rights of tenant.
Every tenant shall have the following rights —
(a) the right to continue in occupation of the premises so long as the rent is paid and the terms of tenancy are observed;
(b) the right to receive proper rent receipts for all payments made;
(c) the right to carry out repairs at the landlord's expense if the landlord fails to do so after due notice;
(d) the right to peaceful enjoyment of the premises without interference from the landlord;
(e) the right to sub-let the premises with the prior written consent of the landlord, which consent shall not be unreasonably withheld.

14. Obligations of tenant.
Every tenant shall —
(a) pay the rent regularly and within the time specified;
(b) keep the premises in good condition and carry out day-to-day maintenance;
(c) not make any structural changes to the premises without the prior written consent of the landlord;
(d) not use the premises for any illegal purpose;
(e) allow the landlord to inspect the premises at reasonable times after giving prior notice of at least twenty-four hours.

15. Procedure for eviction.
(1) No Court shall entertain any suit for eviction of a tenant except on the grounds specified in Section 5.
(2) The landlord shall serve a notice of eviction on the tenant specifying the ground or grounds on which eviction is sought and calling upon the tenant to remedy the breach, if any, within fifteen days.
(3) If the tenant fails to remedy the breach within the specified period, the landlord may file a suit for eviction in the Court having jurisdiction.
(4) The Court shall not grant an order of eviction without giving the tenant a reasonable opportunity to be heard.

16. Rent Authorities.
(1) The State Government shall appoint Rent Authorities for such local areas as it may specify.
(2) The Rent Authority shall have jurisdiction to —
    (a) fix standard rent on application by the landlord or tenant;
    (b) determine disputes regarding repairs and maintenance;
    (c) issue certificates of eviction on specified grounds.
(3) Appeals against orders of the Rent Authority shall lie to the Rent Tribunal.

17. Prohibition of unlawful sub-letting.
(1) No tenant shall sub-let the premises or any part thereof without the prior written consent of the landlord.
(2) Where a tenant sub-lets the premises without consent, the landlord may apply to the Controller for recovery of possession.
(3) Provided that where a tenant has sub-let the premises with the landlord's knowledge for a period exceeding two years without objection, it shall be deemed that the landlord has given his consent to such sub-letting.

18. Protection during legal proceedings.
Where any proceedings for eviction of a tenant are pending before any Court or the Controller, no order of eviction shall be executed unless all arrears of rent are cleared by the tenant.

19. Appeal.
(1) Any person aggrieved by an order of the Controller may prefer an appeal to the High Court within sixty days of the date of the order.
(2) The High Court may, for good cause shown, admit an appeal after the expiry of the said period of sixty days.

20. Penalty.
Any landlord who —
(a) recovers rent in excess of the standard rent or permitted increases;
(b) refuses to issue rent receipts to the tenant;
(c) harasses the tenant or interferes with the tenant's peaceful enjoyment of the premises;
shall be punishable with imprisonment for a term which may extend to three months, or with fine which may extend to five thousand rupees, or with both.

21. Special provisions for Mumbai.
(1) In the city of Mumbai, the provisions of this Act shall apply with certain modifications as specified in the Schedule to this Act.
(2) In Mumbai, premises let for residential purposes at a monthly rent not exceeding ten thousand rupees shall be governed by this Act.
(3) The Bombay Court of Small Causes shall have jurisdiction to try all suits and proceedings under this Act in the city of Mumbai.

22. Savings.
(1) Nothing in this Act shall affect any right, obligation or liability acquired, accrued or incurred before the commencement of this Act.
(2) Any proceeding pending before any Court at the commencement of this Act shall continue and be disposed of as if this Act had not been passed.

23. Power to make rules.
(1) The State Government may make rules for carrying out the purposes of this Act.
(2) All rules made under this Act shall be laid before the State Legislature.

24. Repeal.
The Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, the Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1987, and any other law relating to rent control in force in any part of the State of Maharashtra is hereby repealed.
